# Report: Climate-Smart Agriculture and Water Efficiency (2026)

## Overview
Agriculture accounts for approximately 89% of India's total freshwater consumption. With climate change rendering the monsoon highly erratic, the agricultural sector is undergoing a massive transformation toward water-use efficiency under the core policy of "Per Drop More Crop."

## Shift away from Water-Intensive Crops
The traditional "Green Revolution" cycle of Paddy (Rice) and Wheat in North-Western India is highly unsustainable under current climate models.
- **Millet Mission (Shree Anna)**: The government is heavily incentivizing the cultivation of Millets (Bajra, Jowar, Ragi). Millets require 70-80% less water than paddy, possess a naturally deep root system, and are highly resilient to thermal stress and drought.
- **Crop Pricing**: Adjustments in the Minimum Support Price (MSP) policy in states like Haryana are actively rewarding farmers who switch from paddy to less water-intensive pulses and oilseeds.

## Precision Micro-Irrigation Technologies
Traditional highly-inefficient "flood irrigation" wastes up to 60% of water through evaporation and deep percolation.
- **Drip and Sprinkler Systems**: Adoption of micro-irrigation systems delivers water directly to the root zone. Subsidized rollouts in Maharashtra and Gujarat have resulted in water savings of 40-50% while simultaneously increasing crop yields by 20%.
- **IoT-based Soil Moisture Sensors**: In 2025, pilot agricultural grids integrated IoT sensors. These systems trigger irrigation pumps only when the soil moisture deficit hits a critical threshold, effectively eliminating over-watering.

## Treated Wastewater Reuse
To alleviate pressure on fresh aquifers, peri-urban agriculture (farming on the outskirts of major cities) is pivoting towards the safe reuse of treated municipal wastewater.
- **Nutrient Recovery**: Treated wastewater provides a reliable, drought-proof irrigation source while also supplying essential macronutrients (Nitrogen, Phosphorus) back to the soil, reducing the need for synthetic chemical fertilizers.

## Key Statistics (2026):
- Agricultural water usage: ~89% of national consumption.
- Water savings via Drip Irrigation: 40-50% compared to flood irrigation.
- Water requirement of Millets: ~70% lower than traditional Paddy.
