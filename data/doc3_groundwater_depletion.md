# Report: Groundwater Depletion and Salinity crisis (Updated 2026)

## Overview
India remains the world's largest consumer of groundwater, extracting approximately 245 billion cubic meters (BCM) annually. Climate-induced rainfall variations, combined with deep subsidized agricultural extraction, have pushed aquifers to the brink.

## The North-Western Groundwater Crisis
- **Aquifer Stress (Punjab/Haryana)**: Driven by the water-intensive Paddy-Wheat crop cycle, groundwater in parts of Punjab is now depleting at an alarming rate of 0.8 to 1.2 meters per year. Over 78% of assessment units in Punjab are categorized as "over-exploited."
- **Recharge Deficit**: Flashy monsoons trigger rapid runoff, allowing 30% less time for rainwater to percolate into the critical Alluvial Aquifer System (AAS) compared to the 1990s.
- **Baseflow Disconnection**: Excessive pumping has lowered water tables so severely that rivers like the Yamuna and Gomti no longer receive groundwater "baseflow" during the dry season, converting perennial rivers into seasonal streams.

## Coastal Aquifers and Salinity Intrusion
Accelerated Sea-Level Rise (SLR) and increased abstraction are driving saltwater intrusion along India's 7,500 km coast.
- **Krishna-Godavari Delta**: Saltwater has permanently contaminated aquifers up to 12 km inland, destroying multi-generational farming communities.
- **The Sunderbans & Eastern Coast**: The increased frequency of category 4+ cyclones in the Bay of Bengal drives storm surges that repeatedly inundate shallow freshwater ponds and coastal aquifers with saline water, crippling local agriculture.

## Mitigation and Adaption Policy
- **Atal Bhujal Yojana 2.0**: The updated central sector scheme mandates strict demand-side management, deploying IoT-based smart water meters mapping block-level extraction in real-time.
- **Crop Diversification**: State policies are aggressively pushing farmers away from Paddy toward Millets (Shree Anna), which require 70% less water footprint.

## Key Statistics (2026 Baseline):
- Total Annual Groundwater Extraction: ~245 BCM.
- Groundwater used for Agriculture: ~89%.
- Rate of decline in Punjab: 0.8 to 1.2 meters/year.
- Salinity intrusion in KG Delta: up to 12 km inland.
