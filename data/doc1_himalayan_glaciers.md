# Report: Impact of Climate Change on Himalayan Glaciers and Surface Runoff (Updated 2025-2026)

## Overview
The Himalayan-Hindu Kush (HKH) region, known as the "Third Pole," is undergoing unprecedented changes due to global temperature increases crossing the 1.5°C threshold in specific sub-regions. With over 50,000 glaciers, the region stores crucial freshwater for over 1.9 billion people across South Asia.

## Glacier Retreat and Mass Balance
Recent data (2025) from the Wadia Institute of Himalayan Geology (WIHG) and ISRO's cryosphere satellites indicates an accelerated retreat rate.
- **Indus Basin**: Glaciers like the Siachen and Gangotri are retreating at rates between 10 to 35 meters per year. Siachen specifically has shown a severe thinning rate of ~0.65 meters water equivalent (w.e.) per year.
- **Mass Balance**: Ablation drastically exceeds accumulation. By 2060, it is projected that ~35% of glacier ice volume could be lost under the updated SSP3-7.0 climate scenario.
- **Black Carbon**: Deposition of black carbon (soot) from the Indo-Gangetic plains has accelerated surface melting by altering the albedo effect by nearly 15% in the central Himalayas.

## Impact on River Discharge
Glacier-fed rivers (Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra) are currently experiencing a dramatic "meltwater pulse" (transient increase in discharge). Peak water is expected to hit a sharp decline around 2045.
- **Seasonality Shifts**: The timing of peak runoff has shifted from late spring to early spring. Melt is occurring 3-4 weeks earlier than historical averages, exacerbating water scarcity during the peak summer (Kharif) sowing season.
- **Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOFs)**: Following the devastating 2023 Sikkim floods, CWC reports indicate the number of high-risk proglacial lakes has increased by 14% over the last decade, posing catastrophic risks to multi-billion-dollar hydro-power projects in Uttarakhand, Sikkim, and Arunachal Pradesh.

## Key Statistics (2026 Baseline):
- Projected glacier loss (HKH Region): 40% to 70% by 2100 under high-emission scenarios.
- Contribution of glacier melt to Indus basin streamflow: ~42% (compared to ~12% for the Ganga).
- Retreat rate of Gangotri: ~30 meters/year.
