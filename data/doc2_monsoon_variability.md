# Report: Monsoon Variability and its Impact on Ground and Surface Water (Updated 2026)

## Overview
The Indian Summer Monsoon (ISM) historically provides ~75-80% of India's annual precipitation. Climate change has fundamentally altered the periodicity, intensity, and spatial distribution of the ISM, transitioning it from a predictable cycle to a highly erratic weather manifestation.

## Changing Precipitation Patterns
- **Intensity vs. Frequency**: IMD (Indian Meteorological Department) data from 2025 confirms a 4-fold increase in the frequency of "extreme daily rainfall events" (>150mm/day) over central and peninsular India since 1950. Conversely, "light to moderate" rainfall days have declined by 18%.
- **Monsoon Onset and Withdrawal**: The South-West (SW) monsoon now consistently displays an erratic onset and a delayed withdrawal. States like Maharashtra, Kerala, and Rajasthan are experiencing violent unseasonal October-November rains, destroying mature crop yields.
- **Micro-droughts**: Even during the active monsoon season, there is an increase in the duration of "break days" (dry spells), leading to flash droughts in historically wet regions.

## Regional Water Impacts
- **Gangetic Plains**: The shift toward short, intense rainfall bursts prevents effective percolation. High surface runoff causes devastating multi-state flooding (e.g., Bihar and Assam 2024-2025) while paradoxically leaving the vast alluvial aquifers under-recharged.
- **Aridity Expansion**: The Standardized Precipitation Evapotranspiration Index (SPEI) for 2025 shows severe drought creep into the Southern Peninsula, with Vidarbha and Rayalaseema seeing a 22% spike in aridity.

## Surface Storage Resilience
Reservoirs in the Krishna and Godavari basins are silting up significantly faster due to intense rainfall runoff eroding topsoil.
- **Storage Loss**: CWC estimates indicate these major reservoirs are losing effective storage capacity by ~1.5% annually.
- **Dam Management Crisis**: "Erratic inflows" make rule-curve management extremely difficult, forcing sudden dam gate openings that trigger downstream flash floods.

## Key Statistics (2026 Baseline):
- Extreme rainfall frequency: 4-fold increase over central India (1950-2025).
- Number of dry spell days (Break days): Increased by ~20 days per decade in certain southern districts.
- Reduction in moderate rainfall days: 18% decline nationally.
