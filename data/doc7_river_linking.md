# Report: The National River Linking Project and Climate Extremes (2026 Perspective)

## Overview
The National Perspective Plan (National River Linking Project - NRLP) is India's most ambitious, multi-decade hydrological engineering mega-project. It aims to transfer water from "surplus" river basins (primarily the Brahmaputra and Ganga) to "deficit" basins in western and peninsular India.

## Climate Change as an Accelerant
Historically viewed primarily as an irrigation and drought-proofing project, the NRLP is increasingly justified as a climate adaptation mechanism.
- **Managing Extremes**: By interconnecting rivers via massive canal systems, engineers aim to capture the dangerously high flood peaks (caused by extreme rainfall events) in the north/east and divert them to the drought-stricken southern peninsula.
- **Ken-Betwa Link**: The first major link, transferring water from the Ken river in Madhya Pradesh to the Betwa river in Uttar Pradesh, specifically targets the climate-vulnerable, drought-prone Bundelkhand region.

## Ecological and Feasibility Concerns
While mathematically viable, the ecological cost of river interlinking in an era of climate unpredictability is heavily contested.
- **Loss of "Surplus"**: Climate change is rapidly altering hydrology. Glacial retreat and erratic monsoons mean that basins historically classified as "surplus" might face deficits in the coming decades, rendering multi-billion dollar transfer infrastructure stranded.
- **Biodiversity Impact**: The construction of high dams and deep canals submerges pristine forest reserves—such as the Panna Tiger Reserve in the Ken-Betwa project—destroying vital carbon sinks just when they are most needed to mitigate global warming.
- **Coastal Estuaries**: Diverting massive amounts of river flow before it reaches the ocean disrupts the delicate salinity balance in coastal estuaries (like the Sunderbans), severely impacting marine life and mangrove resilience.

## Key Statistics (2026):
- Scale of NRLP: Envisages 30 links connecting 37 Himalayan and Peninsular rivers.
- Target of Ken-Betwa Link: Provide irrigation to 1.06 million hectares in Bundelkhand.
- Core ecological risk: Alteration of historical "surplus" basin hydrology by 2050 due to climate shift.
